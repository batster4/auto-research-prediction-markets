How ma_crossover works

The strategy computes a 30-tick moving average of price_yes for each market. It buys YES when the current price drops 19.1% below that moving average. This is a deep-discount contrarian signal: the market has sold off significantly relative to its recent trend, and the strategy bets on reversion.

Why 19.1% works: A 30-tick MA with a 19.1% discount is extremely selective — only 37% of markets ever show a dip deep enough to trigger entry. When they do, the entry price is typically very low: the median entry price is just $0.24 (implied probability 24%). This means the strategy is buying deep out-of-the-money options — cheap bets that pay off big when they hit. The win rate is only 34–38%, but winners average $0.61 while losers average only $0.25 — a 2.4:1 win/loss ratio. This is a classic positive-skew strategy.

The remarkable fact: Test metrics are better than train metrics across the board — higher Sharpe (0.090 vs 0.061), higher ROC (21.9% vs 14.7%), higher profit factor (1.44 vs 1.26), and lower drawdown ($2.13 vs $7.59). This is rare and indicates genuine edge rather than overfitting.
