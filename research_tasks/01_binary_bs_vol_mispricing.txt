Approach 1 — Binary / Black–Scholes style (5-minute BTC Up/Down windows)

Context: Each 5-minute window behaves like a short-dated binary option on whether spot finishes above/below the strike. As time-to-expiry shrinks, even small BTC moves can swing fair probability; Polymarket YES prices can lag or overshoot.

Execution constraints (enforced by the backtest engine):
- Resolution cutoff: the last 60 seconds of each market are REMOVED — your strategy cannot see or trade on near-resolution prices (no buying at price=0 near expiry).
- Slippage: $0.005 per share is added to your entry price.
- Taker fees: Polymarket fee schedule is applied on every entry.
- You see ONLY price_yes and timestamp — never the outcome.
- Metrics are computed on a 70% TRAIN split (30% held out for testing).

Dataset: Canonical CSV (high-frequency rows: timestamp, market_id, price_yes, outcome).
price_yes is the implied YES probability from snapshots; outcome is resolved Up=1 / Down=0.
Dataset base rate is ~50% YES. Markets have ~100-120 ticks over 5 minutes.

Goal: Propose strategy parameter sets that find genuine edge BEFORE resolution, not by exploiting near-expiry prices. Consider:
(a) Buy YES when your estimated fair probability exceeds the market price by a sufficient margin (relative_value / early_threshold).
(b) Exploit momentum or mean-reversion patterns in the first portion of the 5-minute window.
(c) Use MA-crossover or momentum signals to detect when a market is trending early.
(d) Vary entry timing (early vs mid-window) and threshold levels.
(e) Think about how vol near expiry affects fair value — but remember you can't trade in the last 60s.

Available strategy types: threshold, momentum, early_threshold, mean_reversion, relative_value, ma_crossover, hold.
Translate your ideas into concrete numeric params for these templates. Explore diverse combinations.
