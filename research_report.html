How gamma_scalp works

This strategy treats 5-minute BTC prediction markets as binary options. Binary option gamma (sensitivity of delta to the underlying) peaks when the option is near at-the-money (price ≈ 0.50). In that regime, small moves in BTC cause disproportionately large swings in the option price — the market tends to overreact.

The strategy waits for the price to be within ±0.11 of 0.50 (the ATM band), then watches for a dip of ≥13% from the recent 4-tick high. It only enters between 28%–72% of time-to-expiry (avoids the noisy start and the illiquid end). When those conditions align — near-ATM, post-dip, mid-life — it buys YES, betting that the overreaction will revert.

Why it works here: The ≥13% dip threshold is fairly aggressive, filtering to 438 out of 1012 train markets (43% entry rate). The hit rate of 55% combined with nearly symmetric wins/losses (~$0.50 each) generates a small but consistent edge. The test set confirms the pattern holds out-of-sample with a 52.4% hit rate and +$4.00 PnL.
